To Silent Hill 2,

  • June 1999 - Silent Hill 2 was starting its development after the first game was complete and released. This was some time around February. Since Silent Hill was a financial success, Konami is moving forward with a sequel. Team Silent also wanted to work on a sequel, but they were frustrated with the limitations of the Playstation during the development of Silent Hill and aimed to seek more creative freedom with the Playstation 2. Konami assigned Team Silent the role for developing the sequel. Team Silent made some changes to some team members' roles. Takayoshi Sato eturns to create computer-generated graphics, character designs, create the premise of the game while taking inspiration from Fyodor Dostoyevsky's Crime and Punishment, and lead the main writing for the game's story along with Hiroyuki Owaku who was the enemy programmer for Silent Hill. Masahiro Ito was a background desinger for Silent Hill was the monster designer of the sequel. He wanted the monsters to inccorperate a element of humanity, he based the distored movements and contorsions based off drunk people and tentitive childern. Akira Yamaoka returned to compose the soundtrack and served as the sounds designer of the game. He wanted to suprise players with the use of sounds and use selective moments to introduce silence in the game. He wanted to avoid the typical sounds that can be heard from something like Resident Evil and go for something that will challenge the imagination.Akihiro Imamura was the producer of the game and help brought 30 people from Konami Computer Entertainment Tokyo to work on Silent Hill 2. Increasing a small team of 10 people to 50-60 people. The story idealized first then the game was developed out of that. The team wanted to focus on psycological horror and wanted the player to reflect the evil of Silent Hill. They wanted to combine the elements of horror and drama to create an atmosphere that will horrify players.

Reception


When the original copies of the Playstation 2 versions were released, the game was generally highly rated. As well as the Xbox releases. However the PC port was recived mixed reviews. As for the game itself, it was priased for its plot, chilling atmosphere, soundtrack, graphics and its gameplay. All of theses elements was a good push in the right direction for survival horror games. At the time, reviewers thought the game was a worthy sequal to the first game but they thought it wasn't better then the original due to the lack of traditoinal horror elements that were present in the first game, there was more ammo compaired to the first game, and some monster can be easily avoided. Some commented on the fog of Silent Hill created a sense of claustraphbia while others may call it a limitation of the graphical capabilities of the Playstation 2. The game was praised for its transitions to the computer generated graphics cutscenes to in-game cutscenes. Character animations where consered realistic at the time. The voice acting was had a mixed respose where reviewers thought the voice acting was well done due to the improved scripted while others thought it was the voice acting was incosistant with some of it line deliveries. Most of the reviewers loved the monster designs. As for the Xbox release, the game was simliar to the original Playstation 2 version except for the "Born from a Wish" side-scenario found in this version of the game. "Born from a Wish" recived mostly mixed reviews. Others called it an accpetible extra while others called it a demo. The PC port recived mixed reviews due to keyboard controls and camera issues. 9 years later Silent Hill 2 and 3 were given a remaster that help to improve the graphics with the new graphical capabitlies of the Playstation 3 and Xbox 360 and released them under Silent Hill HD Collection. The release was not well recived to critics and fans on release and today. They a majority of people disliked the new voice acting, it suffered masive framerate issues, crashes, some of the next textures that they added to the game is worse than the original, graphical glitches and cleared most of the fog which ruins the atmopshere of Silent Hill. Most of the techincal and visual bugs where fixed a couple of years later on Silent Hill 2 but only on the Playstation 3 version and nothing was changed for Silent Hill 3. The way that the games turned out liked that it was because the games where outsourced to a different company to remaster the game and they were handed an version of Silent Hill 2 and 3 with unfinished code because Konami somehow lost the complete builds of those games. Silent Hill 2 is considered one of the best Playstation 2 games or the best video games of all time to many major video game critic outlet such as IGN, Game Informer, Wired News, GamePro and others.
